Welcome to my top 5 list of landmarks to go when you are in london!

Trafalgar Square

Trafalgar Square has a unique role in London’s history, being the place of community gatherings, political demonstrations, and victory parades. With its name commemorating the Battle of Trafalgar, the spot is home to the symbolic Nelson’s Column. A lot of tourist comes around to appreciate its history, admiring the fountains and surroundings. The statues of the 2 lions symbolises this iconic place.

Hyde Park

As big as 350 acres, Hyde Park have over 4000 trees, a lake, meadow and flower garden. With a lot of opportunities for a perfect photo, Hyde Park is usually the perfect place to get an Instagram photo, and for some people, the most beautiful place in London.

Buckingham Palace

You cannot come to London without visiting the Buckingham Palace. This used to be the place where the Queen resides not too long ago before her death. But still, you should check out one of the many tours that are available, explore all the rooms, admire and enjoy the beautiful gardens. Also, the beautiful and elegant scenery around the front gates is also the perfect place to get a picture or two.

Big Ben

Towering above the Palace of Westminster is the world’s most famous clock, Big Ben. It has appeared in many iconic films and the sound of its chimes is iconic throughout in British history. How could you possibly resist taking a picture? How could you not see the tower from afar to not take a picture with it.

Tower Bridge

Perhaps one of the most iconic London landmarks, this bridge is frequently photographed. With a classy brick look to it and light blue accent conplimenting it, it sure does look elegant from afar. Again one of the best landmark for pictures to be posted on Instagram.
